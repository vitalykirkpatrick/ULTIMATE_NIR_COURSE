# Glossary: Lesson 1

- **ALCOA+:** An acronym for the principles of data integrity: Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate, plus Complete, Consistent, Enduring, and Available.

- **Analyst:** A professional who not only performs scientific tests but also interprets the data, ensures its quality, and communicates its meaning to the broader organization.

- **Data Ecosystem:** The complete flow of data from its generation (from a physical sample) to its ultimate use in a business decision, encompassing the physical, digital, and business worlds.

- **Data Integrity:** The maintenance and assurance of the accuracy and consistency of data over its entire life-cycle. It is a critical aspect of the design, implementation, and usage of any system which stores, processes, or retrieves data.

- **Sample Preparation:** The process of treating a sample before it is analyzed to ensure that it is homogeneous, representative, and in a suitable form for the instrument. This is a critical step that directly impacts the quality of the final result.
