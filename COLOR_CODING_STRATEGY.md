# COLOR-CODING STRATEGY - MINIMAL & STRATEGIC

## **Core Principle: 90% WHITE TEXT**

Based on analysis of previous successful lesson examples, color should be used **EXTREMELY SPARINGLY**. Most text (90%) should remain WHITE for maximum readability and professional appearance.

---

## **When to Use Color (10% of text)**

### **1. Section Headers**
- "KEY CONCEPTS", "KEY CHALLENGES", "TREND 1", etc.
- Color: Cyan (#00D9FF)
- Size: 24-32px, bold
- Always colored

### **2. Icons**
- All icons should be cyan (#00D9FF)
- Size: 20-30px
- Line-art style with subtle glow
- Consistent across all slides

### **3. Key Words in Bullets (1-2 words ONLY)**
- Color ONLY the most important 1-2 words per bullet point
- Rest of the bullet stays WHITE
- Colors:
  - **Cyan (#00D9FF)** - Primary concepts, main terms
  - **Purple/Magenta (#B87FFF)** - Technical details (sparingly)
  - **Orange/Gold (#FFB800)** - Warnings, critical alerts ONLY

**Examples:**
- ✅ GOOD: "• **Viscosity** & Homogeneity" (only "Viscosity" colored)
- ❌ BAD: "• **Viscosity** & **Homogeneity**" (too much color)
- ✅ GOOD: "• Miniaturization (Handhelds)" (NO color, all white)
- ✅ GOOD: "• **Temperature** Control" (only "Temperature" orange)

### **4. Warning Boxes**
- Box header in orange/gold (#FFB800)
- Example: "CRITICAL ALERT"
- Body text inside box stays WHITE

### **5. Emphasis Words**
- Single words that need strong emphasis
- Examples: "NEVER", "ALWAYS", "CRITICAL"
- Use sparingly (1-2 per slide max)
- Color: Orange/Gold (#FFB800) for warnings

---

## **What Stays WHITE (90% of text)**

### **ALWAYS Keep White:**
1. **All body paragraphs** - Full sentences and explanations
2. **All supporting words** - and, vs, the, is, of, in, etc.
3. **Most of each bullet point** - Only 1-2 key words colored
4. **All full sentences** - Never color multiple words in a sentence
5. **All descriptive text** - Explanations, definitions, descriptions
6. **Most bullet points** - Many bullets have NO color at all

**Examples from Previous Lessons:**
- "• Miniaturization (Handhelds)" - NO color, all white ✅
- "• Cloud Connectivity & IoT" - NO color, all white ✅
- "• AI & Deep Learning" - NO color, all white ✅
- "• **Hyperspectral** Imaging" - Only "Hyperspectral" colored ✅

---

## **Color Meanings**

When color IS used, it should have consistent meaning:

| Color | Hex Code | Usage | Examples |
|:------|:---------|:------|:---------|
| **Cyan** | #00D9FF | Primary concepts, main terms, section headers | "**Viscosity**", "KEY CONCEPTS", "**Accuracy**" |
| **Purple/Magenta** | #B87FFF | Technical details, secondary concepts (rare) | "**Transflectance**", "**Homogeneity**" |
| **Orange/Gold** | #FFB800 | Warnings, critical alerts, limitations | "**NEVER**", "CRITICAL ALERT", "**Temperature**" |
| **White** | #FFFFFF | ALL other text (90%) | Body text, explanations, most bullets |

---

## **Key Concepts Panel - Restrained Color**

Even in the Key Concepts panel (where color is most used), restraint is key:

**Format:**
- Header: "KEY CONCEPTS" in cyan or white
- Each bullet: Icon (cyan) + Text (mostly white with 1-2 colored words)

**Examples:**
- "• **Viscosity** & Homogeneity" (only "Viscosity" cyan)
- "• **Transmission** vs. **Transflectance**" (key terms cyan, "vs." white)
- "• **Preventing** Sedimentation" (only "Preventing" colored)
- "• **Temperature** Control" (only "Temperature" orange)

**NOT like this:**
- ❌ "• **Viscosity** & **Homogeneity**" (too much color)
- ❌ "• **Transmission** vs. **Transflectance**" (if all words colored)

---

## **Rules of Thumb**

### **The "If Unsure, Keep White" Rule**
- When in doubt about whether to color a word, keep it WHITE
- Less is more
- Color should be surprising and impactful, not overwhelming

### **The "One Per Bullet" Rule**
- Maximum 1-2 colored words per bullet point
- Many bullets have ZERO colored words
- This is intentional and correct

### **The "No Sentence Coloring" Rule**
- NEVER color multiple words in the same sentence
- Full sentences stay WHITE
- Only single key terms get colored

### **The "90/10 Rule"**
- 90% of text should be white
- Only 10% should have color
- If more than 10% is colored, you're using too much

## **Common Mistakes to Avoid**

### ❌ **Mistake 1: Coloring Too Many Words**
**Bad:** "• **Decisions** Depend on **Data**"
**Good:** "• Decisions Depend on **Data**"

### ❌ **Mistake 2: Coloring Supporting Words**
**Bad:** "• **Accuracy** **vs** **Precision**"
**Good:** "• **Accuracy** vs **Precision**"

### ❌ **Mistake 3: Coloring Full Sentences**
**Bad:** "**NIR** can **NEVER** be more **accurate** than the **Reference Method**"
**Good:** "NIR can **NEVER** be more accurate than the Reference Method"

### ❌ **Mistake 4: Using Color When Not Needed**
**Bad:** "• **Miniaturization** (Handhelds)"
**Good:** "• Miniaturization (Handhelds)" (NO color needed)

---

## **Quality Check Questions**

Before finalizing any slide, ask:

1. **Is 90% of the text white?** If not, remove color.
2. **Are there any bullets with NO color?** There should be many.
3. **Are full sentences colored?** They shouldn't be.
4. **Are supporting words (and, vs, the) colored?** They shouldn't be.
5. **Does the color add clarity or just decoration?** Remove if just decoration.

## **Summary**

**The winning formula:**
- **90% white text** - Most text has NO color
- **1-2 words per bullet** - Maximum colored words per line
- **Many bullets with ZERO color** - This is intentional and correct
- **Color for emphasis ONLY** - Not decoration
- **If unsure, keep white** - Less is more

**This restrained approach:**
- ✅ Looks more professional
- ✅ Is easier to read
- ✅ Focuses attention on what matters
- ✅ Doesn't overwhelm students
