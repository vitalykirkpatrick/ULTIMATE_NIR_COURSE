# Week 1 Quiz: The Big Picture

**Instructions:** Choose the best answer for each question.

---

**1. What is the primary goal of this course?**

a) To teach advanced quantum mechanics
b) To provide a comprehensive understanding of NIR spectroscopy, from theory to practical application
c) To focus solely on the business aspects of NIR
d) To provide a brief overview of various analytical techniques

**2. Which of the following best describes analytical chemistry?**

a) The study of chemical processes in living organisms
b) The development of new chemical compounds
c) The science of obtaining, processing, and communicating information about the composition and structure of matter
d) The study of the rates of chemical reactions

**3. What is the fundamental principle behind NIR spectroscopy?**

a) The interaction of X-rays with matter
b) The absorption of ultraviolet light by molecules
c) The interaction of near-infrared light with molecular vibrations
d) The scattering of light by large particles

**4. Which of the following is NOT a primary component of an NIR spectrometer?**

a) Light source
b) Sample holder
c) Detector
d) Mass spectrometer

**5. What information can be obtained from an NIR spectrum?**

a) The elemental composition of a sample
b) The crystal structure of a material
c) Information about the chemical and physical properties of a sample based on its molecular vibrations
d) The exact molecular weight of a compound

**6. What is the typical wavelength range for NIR spectroscopy?**

a) 100-400 nm
b) 400-700 nm
c) 780-2500 nm
d) 2500-10000 nm

**7. Which of the following is a key advantage of NIR spectroscopy?**

a) It requires extensive sample preparation
b) It is a destructive technique
c) It is slow and not suitable for real-time analysis
d) It is fast, non-destructive, and can measure multiple parameters simultaneously

**8. What does the Beer-Lambert Law relate?**

a) The concentration of a substance to its refractive index
b) The absorbance of light to the concentration of the absorbing substance and the path length of the light
c) The intensity of scattered light to the size of the particles
d) The frequency of a molecular vibration to the bond strength

**9. What is the difference between qualitative and quantitative analysis?**

a) Qualitative analysis identifies what is in a sample, while quantitative analysis measures how much is present
b) Qualitative analysis measures how much is present, while quantitative analysis identifies what is in a sample
c) There is no difference; the terms are interchangeable
d) Qualitative analysis is only used for liquids, while quantitative analysis is only for solids

**10. Which of the following is an example of a real-world application of NIR spectroscopy discussed in Week 1?**

a) DNA sequencing
b) Carbon dating of ancient artifacts
c) Measuring the protein content in wheat
d) Analyzing the composition of stars
